13 Simulating Collaborative Robots in Massive Multi-Agent Game Execution, or
14 SCRIMMAGE, provides a flexible simulation environment for the experimentation
15 and testing of novel mobile robotics algorithms. The development of SCRIMMAGE
16 was inspired by the [Stage](http://playerstage.sourceforge.net/doc/stage-svn/)
17 robotics simulator. Specifically, Stage's ability to efficiently simulate
18 multiple robots and its plugin interface for robotic control, motion models,
19 sensors, and autonomy communication are desirable. However, Stage is inherently
20 a two-dimensional simulator, which limits its usefulness for aerial robotic
21 platforms. SCRIMMAGE provides a three-dimensional robotics environment that can
22 simulate varying levels of sensor and motion model fidelity due to its flexible
23 plugin interface. This allows a robotics researcher to simulate hundreds of
24 aircraft with low-fidelity motion models or tens of aircraft with high-fidelity
25 motion models on a standard consumer laptop. If greater performance is desired,
26 SCRIMMAGE was designed with parallelization in mind, such that it can execute
27 on a cluster.
28 
29 The [Georgia Tech Research Institute (GTRI)](https://gtri.gatech.edu/)
30 supports the development of SCRIMMAGE.
